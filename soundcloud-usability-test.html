RESEARCH

usability test and alternatives - soundcloud

 

Year: 2015

Client: soundcloud

Designer: Jae Yong Lee
Parts of participation: DESIGN RESEARCH, data analysis, report
Participation: 100%

This usability test of a mobile application from SoundCloud aimed for highlighting main usability issues in the iOS application of SoundCloud and suggesting alternatives to resolve it.

BACKGROUND

 

SoundCloud is one of the biggest sound social media platforms that helps users share their public sounds including music, podcast, and audio books, and communicate with over 175 million unique monthly listeners through web, iOS, Android.  As many listeners and creators are using SounCloud everyday, the holistic user experiences through usability, utility are controversial.  User interface in SoundCloud’s website, iOS, and Android is already well considered and designed for satisfying users’ needs, but there are rooms for further improvements to deliver optimal user experiences.

USABILITY TEST

 

Usability tests primarily conducted to figure out what are the users’ issues, not design researchers’ assumption or preconception.

 

Purpose of usability test

 

• Instead of assuming it through my own opinions, listening real anticipated users’ opinions expected to be more convincible.

 

• Holistic user experience cannot be evaluated through this task, but it would be draw a big picture of experience for SoundCloud.

 

• Addressing issues with different perspectives based on users’ opinions, and delivering opportunities helping SoundCloud think in different ways to improve current usability.

<Conducting usability test via SoundCloud iOS application with 5 participants>

QUESTIONS

 

Finding and Managing

 

Find your favorite song (already they have info about the songs) and add it on your new playlist, while follow the artist. And then, check the artist’s profile.

 

Playing

 

While playing, change volume, skip some parts of a song, stop playing, move to another song, and add it on your playlist.

 

New Findings and Sharing

 

Explore new sounds what they are interested to listen and share it with their friends through social media channels.

 

Throughout these tasks, SoundCloud’s main features that Stream, Explore, Collection, Playing for mobile application can be tested. Creating sounds and uploading will not be dealt through this test since only web has these functions.

<List of tasks during usability tests>

TEST RESULTS

 

Users’ confusion levels depending on main functions were shown throughout the levels of unclearness of system structure, ambiguous icons, hidden buttons, and absence of basic functions. Users showed relatively high confusion levels in Stream, Explore, Control, Checking others’ information, and Managing part in comparison with Searching, Playing, and Sharing part. Some of the issues are not easily defined as a simple usability problem, so it should be holistically considered to find out unseen reasons behind these issues.

<The levels of low usability causing confusion by functions>

MAIN ISSUES WITH ALTERNATIVES

 

1. INSUFFICIENT GUIDANCE FOR USER INTERFACE

- INTERACTIVE GUIDANCE: Brining clear understanding of interface for first users.

 

2. LACK OF PERSONALIZED SUGGESTIONS

- Personalized suggestions

 

3. UNCLEAR DIVISION FOR STREAM / EXLPORE SECTION

- Clear division for stream / Explore

1. Insufficient guidance for User Interface

 

For first time users, everything is new, and they need to figure out how an application works and what kinds of services are provided. At the moment, there are a few slides teaching what the main systems are, but it is not sufficient to cover the introduction of interactive interfaces and system structure. Without this guidance, first time users would be confused with some words like Stream, Repost, Collection, as well as the way of using some buttons, e.g., preference button, a button for Artist’s information, and interactive buttons in Playing part.

 

alternative:

Interactive guidance

-Bringing clear understanding of interface for inexperienced users.

 

Considering the fact that user interface of SoundCloud is easy to use after understanding how to handle it, interactive guidance for users who are not familiar with the SoundCloudʼs interface would be helpful. Especially for the terms such as ʻStreamʼ and ʻCollectionʼ, which are not familiar words with the first time users, can be easily understood through the communicative guidance. Interactive buttons in Playing part to change sound or skipping parts of sound, which brought confusion to users can be guided in a simple way.

 

2. Lack of personalized suggestions

 

Four participants wanted to get personalized, and automatic suggestions from SoundCloud system based on their interests while using the application. Finding new sounds is the main reason of using SoundCloud, but it was not easy finding good sounds by user themselves in comparison with SoundCloudʼs website UI.

 

“If this application doesnʼt find new music for me, itʼs not interesting.”

 

One of the users emphasized the importance of finding ʻawesomeʼ sounds when he uses a music application. He reckoned that the most popular sound is most convincible content. Users wanted to listen to sounds that other users mostly interested in.

 

alternative:

tailored suggestions

 

Most of participants desired getting personalized music suggestions automatically depending on what they chose, e.g., based on Like, Playlist, and Repost. Suggestions would make them explore SoundCloudʼs contents more freely, and easily. It would also make users stay in the service more, while being satisfied with SoundCloudʼs service.

 

 

3. Unclear division for Stream / Explore section with ambiguous icons and buttons

 

SoundCloud iOS application does not have clear section for Stream/Explore in comparison with web UI. In the application, Stream includes the explore function through a small button, which is hard to recognize as a button for users. Two participants could not perceive it as a button, and did not push it to figure out what different categories exist in the system. Instead of using it, they used Search function with keywords, e.g. electronic. This way of finding music could not satisfy users’ different desires, so it should be more clarified through a direct way. Text based categorization is also problematic factor for some users, since they could not get direct understanding of which section is interesting. In addition, several icons, buttons, and words disturb the direct understanding of its functions that should be easily understandable.

 

alternative:

Clear division for Stream / Explore with clear UI

 

By dividing Stream and Exploring in a Home section, the structure of Home is much easier to comprehend, as well as easy to perceive Stream and Explore as buttons like others, e.g., Artist’s name.

 

 

Additional opinions

 

1. Managing Collection is restricted

 

Using Playlist and Like is the main way of listening sounds, however there are no basic managing functions in Collection like ʻMove, Delete, and Repeatʼ at the moment. It is true that simple is the best, but sometimes it needs to be more considered in perspective of users who are using this application everyday in their lives.

 

2. Collection / My profile

 

Participants answered that Collection might be a kind of ʻMy Profileʼ page, which is similar with Facebook. However, when they found out actual My profile section in Preference, they become confused because the contents of My profile and Collection did not have almost any differences. SoundCloudʼs iOS application does not provide the profile editing function, but the differences between two sections should be clearer than at the moment.

 

3. Sharing through other social media channels.

 

Sharing through Twitter did not have any problem, but when it came to Facebook, application could not provide personal sharing function, which means that they cannot share sounds with a friend privately. Sharing with all friends is good, but sometime users’ needs are different. Furthermore, when a song shared, there were no indications for the completion, so users wondered that whether it shared well or not.

 

4. Interface for playing section

 

Current interface is consisted with many interactive buttons. Especially, the touch area of stop function is broad, almost everywhere. It would make some problematic situations that one accidently pushes the stop button, and then music just stopped. Additionally, one of the basic function repeating a song, or playlist’ is unavailable, so the users’ needs are unsatisfied.
