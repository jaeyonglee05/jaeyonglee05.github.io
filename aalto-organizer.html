UI / UX

Service design

Personalized study organizer

 

Year: 2014
Designer: Jae Yong Lee, EMMA-SOFIE KUKKONEN, luca acito, nan yang, lotta ahonen
Parts of participation: DESIGN RESEARCH, WORKSHOP, SERVICE DEVELOPEMENT, UI/UX
Participation: 90%

Aalto Organizer is a digital service that helps students register courses and get relevant information on right time. Based on calendar format, Aalto Organizer provides information to support course registrations in more correct and easier ways. Via this service, students can make efficient study plans, share information with schoolmates, and easily enroll right courses. It provides correct and relevant course information and it integrates scattered information from different school websites.

BACKGROUND

 

There are several universities that are using Weboodi, which is a course registration system in Helsinki. This Weboodi system designed more than 10 years ago, and does not deliver optimal user experience for students and universities faculties. Not only this problem, but also course rules governed by universities were problematic, because some of courses have the limitation of number of students who can participate. In this case, professors need to select students by evaluating students, e.g., giving an assignment and selecting students, but it is not enough to satisfy for both; professors need to evaluate each student to be fare which takes time and effort, and students need to do the extra assignment to get in. Moreover, information is scattered around on different websites; students are hard to find appropriate course information. On the top of that, information on websites is often different, so students have troubles to make proper study plans before the courses.

 

RESEARCH

 

PERSONA

 

To understand current situation in a more empathic way as well as to get very initiative concepts in early phase, our team made a persona named Jacob. Through his story, we highlighted current problems and brief future alternatives.

CO-DESIGN WORKSHOP

 

Our team organized a workshop to get creative and fresh ideas from stakeholders. Involving users into a design process is an important aspect of the collaborative design, so we intended to have deeper understandings of users’ perspectives and new insights of our project through this workshop.

STAKEHOLDER JOURNEY MAP

 

We tried to understand holistic processes about our subject, so we analyzed stakeholder journey map in chronological order. Students, Professor and Study coordinator involve in this process closely and they interact each other during this time.  After analyzing different stakeholders’ processes, we could approach on our project while wearing different stakeholders’ shoes.

 

The reason why we considered it crucially was that new system would be utilized not only for students’ enrollment issue, but also professors and study coordinators’ different purposes. E.g. professors need to upload study materials and study coordinators help students who have problems in the process.

CONCEPT

 

Aalto Organizer designed for students’ optimal experiences when they are concerned with the enrollment of courses at Aalto University. As problems highlighted before, too much information are scattered around on different platforms, incorrect information regarding courses, and low accessibility were the main pain points of this project. To solve these issues and to provide a integrated service platform to students, our team designed Aalto Organizer based on calendar format, which is beneficial to check out all information at a glance with smooth user experiences. The new platform make it possible that students can always access relevant information anytime through different devices and they can manage their personalized study plan that would help them set the right courses to have their own development during their study. The user interface design is easy to understand at once and designed in efficient way of managing course schedules as well as getting course information conveniently.

 

First of all, when students log in to the system, they will check directly relevant courses depending on personal data that come from our system. For example, if it is first semester of 2015 in Collaborative and Industrial Design programme, the system automatically show the related courses at the first page. Of course, students can choose other courses by exploring other major programmes through the easy-searching tool or the different major list. To check out more specific information about a course, students can select course name on the calendar. And then, it will show more detailed course information including general description, movie clip of the course, professor information and the name of students who already registered. If students want to put the course on their study plan, they can push a ‘study plan’ button that would help them see all the personally selected courses at the same time. At the registration day, they can get automatic notification from our system and can directly enroll the course by just clicking one button. Aalto Organizer is available for all Internet-based devices including computers, smartphones, and smart tablets.
